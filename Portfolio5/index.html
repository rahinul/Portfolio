Problem

Every insurance company pools funds from policyholders. The more money left in the pool, the more is the company’s profit. It makes it profitable to deny even valid claims.

In turn, customers see insurers as an adversary, not an ally. It leads to a high amount of frauds committed by otherwise law-abiding persons. 24% respondents find insurance fraud acceptable. And anti-fraud measures are costly and hostile.


The result is unfair treatment, bureaucracy, intransparency and prices that are much higher than customers’ expectations.

Our Solution

Teambrella is what insurance have always strived to be: a tool for sharing risks with peers and helping those in need. But this time, we don’t just declare the absence of conflict of interest - we align interests of all parties and make the whole process provably fair.

P2P Payments

Teambrella is based on empathy. And while fairness is subjective we let our users use their empathy and decide what they believe is fair -- be it a team rule, acceptance of a new teammate or the amount of a reimbursement.

Transparency

Teambrella enforces the Golden Rule: “Treat others the way you want to be treated”. If you file a valid claim and some guy ponders about voting to pay you nothing, unfairly, without a good reason - he knows that other teammates would .

Liquid Democracy

Most teammates don’t vote all the time. Each teammate may appoint one or more proxies to vote on his or her behalf. Teammates and proxies who do vote get compensated by the team. Good and fair proxies accumulate.
